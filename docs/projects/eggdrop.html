ü•ö Project Overview

This unit focuses on Impulse, Momentum, and Energy. You will complete two separate egg drop challenges to test your understanding of how to reduce impact forces.


Instead of a formal lab report, you will complete a Google Form Reflection after your drops. You will need to perform calculations regarding the impact velocity and energy of your device.

Submission Requirements

  • Format: Google Form (Link to be provided on drop day).
  • Content: You must have your mass, drop height, and time data ready to enter.
  • Grading: Total 120 Points (60 points per drop).

Drop #1: The Paper Device

Goal: Create a lightweight landing device using minimal materials.

Rules & Restrictions

  • Materials: Paper and Tape ONLY. No glue, staples, or clips.
  • Mass Limit: 15 grams (measured without the egg).
  • Design: Must be unique. No copying other groups.

The 15g Challenge

For every gram over 15g, you will lose 1 point from your grade.

Drop #2: The Homemade Container

Goal: Engineer a robust container to protect the egg from a high fall.

Rules & Restrictions

Required Calculations

For your Google Form reflection, you must calculate the following values for BOTH drops. Assume g = 9.8 m/s¬≤.

1. Velocity & Energy

Velocity without Device (Freefall):

v = √(2gh)

Actual Velocity at Impact:

(Calculate using your drop time t)

vf = g × t

Energy at the Top:

PE = mgh, KE = 0

2. Momentum & Impulse

Momentum (p) at Impact:

p = m × vf

Impulse (J):

The Impulse required to stop the egg is equal to the change in momentum.

J = Δp = m(vf - vi)

Remember: To save the egg, we want to increase the time of impact to decrease the Force (F = J/t).
