Project Objective

Your challenge is to design, build, and test a vehicle powered *only* by the energy stored in one or two standard mousetraps. You will then analyze the physics of your car's motion in a formal lab report.

The Car: Rules & Performance

Group Size & Distance Goal

  • Groups of 1-2 students: Your car must travel a minimum distance of 10.0 meters.
  • Groups of 3 students: Your car must travel a minimum distance of 15.0 meters.

Car Construction Rules

  1. Power: The car must be powered by 1 or 2 standard mousetraps (provided). No other energy sources (batteries, elastic bands, CO2).
  2. Materials: Completely homemade. NO pre-made kits, Legos, K'nex, Erector sets.
  3. Wheels: At least 3 wheels required.
  4. Launch: Start from stationary. No pushing.

Report Requirements

Your lab report must be formatted professionally and include detailed answers to the following sections:

  1. Title Page: Student Name, Partner Name, Date, Title.
  2. Objective (3-5 sentences):
    What was the goal and objective of this project? Did you meet the requirements?
  3. Procedure & Diagrams:
    What materials did you use? Include diagrams of your project with measurements (Top and Side view). Your diagram will need to match your car and include all of your measurements.
  4. Data Tables:
    • Table 1 (Raw): Time to Snap, Distance to Snap, Snap to Stop Time, Snap to Stop Distance.
    • Table 2 (Calculated): Acceleration to Snap, Velocity at Snap, Kinetic Energy at Snap, Acceleration to Stop, Work to Stop.
  5. Sample Calculations:
    For each different equation used, show one sample calculation (Equation used, Work with numbers, Answer).
  6. Physics (1-2 Paragraphs):
    Discuss different concepts in physics (Newton's Laws, Energy, Friction) that go into making a mousetrap car be successful.
  7. Results (5-7 sentences):
    What was the outcome of your project? What were the average values that you found for your car? If you were to modify your car, what would you change? Do you consider your car a success?
  8. Sources:
    List references used for design or physics concepts in a standard format.

5. Physics Theory

To ensure success, we relied on the conservation of energy. The potential energy stored in the spring is the only source of power. By using a long lever arm, we decreased the force applied to the axle but increased the distance over which that force acted. We minimized negative acceleration (a2) by reducing friction with CD wheels, allowing the Kinetic Energy (KE) to carry the car further.
